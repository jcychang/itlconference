Accessible Learning and the Inclusive University: The UMD Perspective

Panelists: Carolyn M. Fink, Ph.D., Paul T. Jaeger, Ph.D., J.D., Dr. Ana Palla-Kane, Megan Masters, Moderator

Lightweight Teams: A Design Pattern for Large, Flipped Intro Classes

Dr. Celine Latulipe

Abstract: In this talk, I will present the concept of “Lightweight Teams”, which has been shown to increase student engagement in introductory programming classes. I will explain how this design pattern can be customized to effectively structure active learning activities in flipped classrooms. I will show the results we have so far, which were the subject of a best paper award at the ACM SIGCSE (Special Interest Group on Computer Science Education) Conference in 2015. I believe using lightweight teams to bring social learning to introductory classes has the potential to reduce student social anxiety, increase retention, improve communication skills and is especially likely to help women and under-represented minorities feel more welcome in the classroom.
