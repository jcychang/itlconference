About the ITL Conference

Adele H. Stamp Student Union

Wanted: Fearless teachers, assistants, and peer mentors!

To teach fearlessly means to embrace the scholarship and art of teaching through reflection, training, and professional development. Decades of research on student learning and achievement has found four essential characteristics of an effective course: excellent content, an inclusive climate, evidence-based teaching practices, and valid assessments. Come present at this year’s ITL Conference and share what makes your teaching fearlessly innovative and effective.

Call for Proposals

Presentation Types

Fearless in Action:
Presenting an overview of how you have innovated and put best practices into play with your teaching.

Research & Evaluation:
Summarizing scholarly work on teaching and learning and the implications of your work.

Demonstration:
Engaging attendees with hands-on activities, tools, and techniques that attendees can apply in their own courses. Discuss how you will engage participants in the session.

Browse our archives to get a sense of the kinds of approaches that have been featured in the past and to get a sense of the diverse faculty innovators who have shared their experiences with their peers.

Presentation Formats

Panel (5-15 minutes per participant)

Submitter suggests a panel topic and identifies co-presenters and moderator, or asks to be matched with related presenters.

Individual Session (15-60 minutes)


Lightning round (5 minutes)

Share your golden nugget idea, practice, or experience in engaging, fast-paced session of mini-presentations.

Poster

(Up to a 75 minute presentation opportunity held during the “Thank a Teacher” Award ceremony; required participation between 2:45-3:15 P.M., optional continuation during wine and cheese reception between 3:45-4:30 P.M.) The Conference will print your poster for you. Details will be provided to those whose proposals are accepted.
